Hexabenzocoronene

Information

Hexa-peri-hexabenzocoronene (HBC) is a polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbon with the molecular formula C42H18.

It consists of a central coronene molecule, with an additional benzene ring fused between each adjacent pair of rings around the periphery.

It is sometimes simply called hexabenzocoronene, however, there are other chemicals that share this less-specific name, such as hexa-cata-hexabenzocoronene.

Various hexabenzocoronenes have been investigated in supramolecular electronics.

The adjacent pairs of benzene rings undergo oxidative electrocyclic reactions and aromatization by oxidation by Iron(III) chloride in nitromethane.
